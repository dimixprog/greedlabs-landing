**Most new tokens launch on a DEX first: it needs no listing approval, trading starts instantly, and early price discovery happens on-chain. Centralized exchange listings usually follow, adding reach once there is traction.** The right sequence depends on your audience, treasury, and listing readiness.

For the mechanics of providing liquidity on each, see [CEX vs. DEX Market Making](/blog/cex-vs-dex-market-making).

## Why DEX first is the common path

- **No gatekeeper** — anyone can create a pool; there's no application or approval.
- **Instant trading** — the token is tradable the moment it exists, aligning with [TGE](/blog/token-launch-checklist).
- **On-chain price discovery** — the community can participate immediately.
- **Evidence for later listings** — a healthy on-chain market strengthens a future CEX application.

The catch: a DEX pool that isn't properly sized and managed leads to painful [slippage](/blog/slippage-explained) and MEV exposure. DEX-first still needs real liquidity, not just a token and a pool.

## Why (and when) CEX first makes sense

- **Reach and credibility** — major exchanges bring a far larger audience.
- **Fiat on-ramps** — easier access for non-crypto-native buyers.

But CEX listings require **approval, fees, and a market making commitment**, and they take time. See [How to Get Listed on a CEX](/blog/how-to-get-listed-on-cex). Launching CEX-first suits teams with existing traction, treasury for listing costs, and relationships already in place.

## How to decide

| Factor | Leans DEX first | Leans CEX first |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Listing readiness | Not yet approved | Approval in hand |
| Treasury | Limited cash | Budget for fees + commitment |
| Audience | Crypto-native, on-chain | Broad / retail |
| Speed | Launch now | Can wait for process |

## The usual answer

For most projects it's **DEX first, then CEX as you scale** — with real liquidity on both when the time comes, and consistent pricing across venues so arbitrage doesn't distort your chart. Size the on-chain market properly using [How Much Liquidity Does Your Token Need Before TGE?](/blog/liquidity-before-tge), and check any unfamiliar terms in the [glossary](/blog/market-making-glossary).
